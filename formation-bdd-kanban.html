Programme

Kanban & BDD 2 days

Differentiation, Complexity & Cynefin (Discussion, Workshop)

Every project involves doing something new; something that we’ve never done before.

In this session we see if we can identify the differentiators for our companies and projects. These are the places where requirements will be harder to understand and implement, and where practices which allow us to respond to discoveries are essential.

Cynefin is a framework for helping us work out if we are dealing with complexity or if work is merely complicated, and to consider different approaches for each.

The Deliberate Discovery Workshop

Using real problems encountered on different projects, we look at two ways of improving processes. Deliberate Discovery helps us discover information early, before making commitments, while Real Options helps us to be resilient in the face of the unexpected. This workshop is a great way to think about root causes, as well as providing a clear, easy retrospective format for any difficulty.

The Pennies Game

Now that we have a better understanding of complexity in software, we know that a lot of our work involves learning and discovery. The Pennies Game will show us the difference between working as individuals, and working as a team, when getting feedback quickly and sharing knowledge are the most important things you can be doing.

Kanban 101 (Talk)

Kanban practices are similar to those of Agile, but it has a slightly different focus. Using principles from Lean Product Development (as opposed to Lean Manufacturing), it handles both new and familiar work gracefully, while allowing expedited bugs to interrupt the workflow.

This session provides a simple overview of Kanban, its values and its practices.

The Kanban Game

In this game, we look at the effects of limiting Work in Progress, and the signals that help people know what work to do next, when to stop and help someone else, and when to simply take a step back and reflect on the system as a whole.

BDD and Feature Injection (Mixed talk and workshop)

Behaviour-Driven Development helps technical and business-focused team members to have useful conversations about the requirements, then carry the language of those conversations into the code, often turning the scenarios discussed into tests.This session is completely non-technical and focuses on effective conversations – the real benefit of BDD. This helps teams quickly focus on differentiators, express familiar work in familiar terms, and feed back understanding so that missed scope is spotted early

Review

A look back at everything learned so far.

Closer Capabilities (Workshop)

What are the capabilities of a Monopoly game? How about a trading platform? Amazon’s book store? A washing machine? Twitter?

Now that we’ve identified the capabilities, can we phrase scenarios around them? What are the different behaviours involved in each capability, and what are the edge-cases needed to cope with exceptions?

Which scenarios would make good candidates for automation, and what would you do to test the others?

Visualise your Workflow

In this session, we look at different ways to visualise the workflow - including working out what the workflow is in the first place!

Retrospectives and Futurespectives

One of Kanban’s core practices is: “improve collaboratively, evolve experimentally”.

In this session we look at some simple ways of doing that, and try out a futurespective around what a Kanban adoption might look like.

Bio

Elizabeth Keogh

Liz Keogh currently works as an independent Agile Coach and trainer based in London. She is a well-known blogger and international speaker, a core member of the BDD community and a contributor to a number of open-source projects including JBehave.

Liz has written over a dozen workshops on subjects as diverse as story-writing, haiku poetry, systems thinking, effective personal feedback and OO development, and has extensive experience in delivering courses internationally, having worked in a number of different countries including China and India.

Liz has a strong technical background with over 10 years’ experience in delivering and coaching others to deliver large-scale enterprise applications, which she now combines with a focus on psychology, NLP and adult learning. She has pioneered the application of learning models in measuring Agile maturity and coaching progress, and most of her work now focuses on Agile and architectural organizational transformations, and the use of positive language, targets and metrics in facilitating change.

Liz Keogh is a recepient of the Gordon Pask Award at Agile 2010.
